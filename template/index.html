Information

The virus was initially discovered in Wuhan, China, in December 2019.

Close contact and aerosols and respiratory droplets expelled when talking, breathing, or otherwise exhaling, as well as those generated by coughs or sneezes, are the most common ways for the virus to spread.

  • Even if otehrs don't appear any Covid symptoms, keep a safe distance from them (at least 1 metre).
  • In public, wear a mask, especially indoors or when physical separation is impossible.
  • Hands should be washed often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub.
  • When it's your turn, get vaccinated. Follow local vaccine recommendations.
  • Stay home if you feel unwell.
  • Seek medical help if you have a fever, cough, or difficulty breathing. This safeguards you and stops viruses and other illnesses from spreading.
